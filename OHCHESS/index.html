Announcement from OCA

I am submitting the following revised motion to amend the OCA by-laws to take effect at the Ohio Chess Congress of 2020. This amendment can be added to Article 3 Section 2 of the by-laws.

All players competing in any designated OCA event shall receive a free one year OCA membership. The organizer of all OCA tournaments shall be required to submit to the OCA upon completion of the tournament a fee of $5 per player; along with a list of players and their home and email addresses. The $5 fee shall be included as part of the entry fee for out of state players as well as Ohio residents. Organizations or individuals not conferred as members through tournament participation may choose to join OCA and purchase a one year membership for $5.

The amendment will be changed as a compromise as follows :

1. The amendment will not take affect until the event of the Congress in year 2020.
2. No participant shall be required to pay more than $5 per annum.
3. Life members will not be required to pay $5 to the Ohio Chess Association for any event.

Regards,

Mike Joelson

Current and former Ohioans combine to win USATN

John Randolph (Cincinnati) and Scott Ramer (Columbus) paired up with former Ohioans Brian Lilly (now in Wisconsin) and Scot Henderson (now in Illinois) to form the J'adoobie Brothers. Glen Gratz from Illinois rounded out the team. They took clear first in the US Amateur Team North with 4.5/5. Keep an eye on uschess.org for a full article soon.

Jason Wang shares first at Barber Tournament of K-8 Champions

Jason Wang shared first at the Barber Tournament of K-8 Champions with Robert Shlyakhtenko. Both players had 5/6, though Robert Shlyakhtenko had better tiebreaks and earned a $5000 scholarship.

WHO WE ARE

The purpose of our association is to foster the study and pursuit of the game of chess by conducting tournaments for players, sponsoring teams to represent the state of Ohio in interstate and international play, promote chess organizations in the state of Ohio, and the overall general promotion of chess.
